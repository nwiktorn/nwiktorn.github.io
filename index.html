02 / How I Work

No mystery. No handholding.
Just a clear process.

Every project goes through the same four stages. Brief to delivery — no gaps, no surprises, no "can you just add one more thing" after the file's been sent to print.

01 / BRIEF

Understand before you design.

I ask the questions most designers skip. What's the actual problem? Who's the buyer? What does "on brand" mean to you — and does it match what your brand actually is right now?

The intake is thorough on purpose. Garbage in, garbage out — and I'd rather spend an hour on the brief than redo a finished file.

Discovery call · Written brief · Project scope
02 / CONCEPT

One strong direction. Not three weak ones.

I don't do the "three options and a prayer" routine. You get one well-considered direction — researched, reasoned, presented with context.

If it needs a pivot, we pivot. But I'd rather defend a smart choice than overwhelm you with alternatives designed to hedge my bets.

Moodboard · Initial concepts · Rationale
03 / DEVELOP

Refinement, not guesswork.

One round of focused feedback, then I develop. No endless loops. No "let's try this and see." We're moving toward the final, not orbiting it.

This is where the system gets built — the logic, the spacing, the colour rules that make it hold together at every size and in every format.

Feedback loop · Asset development · System building
04 / DELIVER

Files that actually work. Everywhere.

Print-ready PDFs. Web-optimised exports. Brand guidelines your future self will thank you for. I handle the technical side so the vendor, developer or printer doesn't come back with questions.

You get the full package — not just the pretty version.

Production files · Export formats · Brand guidelines
Before we start.

Some projects wrap in a week. Others take four. Scope sets the timeline — I'll tell you exactly where yours lands before we begin. One client at a time, real deadlines, structured revisions. No surprises.
